## Disease Group Comparisons

### GSE36700: Synovial Biopsies (n=25)

Senescence scores compared across 5 disease groups:

| Comparison | N (SLE) | N (Other) | Effect Size | Mann-Whitney U | p-value | Significance |
|------------|---------|-----------|-------------|----------------|---------|--------------|
| SLE vs OA | 4 | 5 | +1.495 | 18 | 0.064 | Trend |
| SLE vs RA | 4 | 7 | -0.548 | 11 | 0.649 | Not significant |
| SLE vs MIC | 4 | 5 | +1.119 | 16 | 0.190 | Not significant |

- **Kruskal-Wallis (all groups)**: H = 12.69, **p = 0.005** (significant overall difference)
- **ROC (SLE vs non-SLE)**: AUC = 0.679

The significant Kruskal-Wallis (p=0.005) confirms senescence heterogeneity across disease groups in synovial tissue. SLE vs OA trending effect (+1.495, p=0.064) warrants validation in larger tissue cohorts.

## SenMayo Pathway Decomposition

SASP category genes show higher mean expression than canonical cell-cycle arrest genes across all 3 datasets with direct SenMayo matching. SASP/Canonical ratio exceeds 1.0, suggesting that in SLE-relevant tissues, the inflammatory secretory phenotype — not cell-cycle arrest — dominates the senescence program.

## Future Directions

1. **Gene ID harmonization**: Implementing Ensembl-to-HUGO mapping will enable direct SenMayo scoring across all 19 datasets, strengthening the current 4-dataset anchor analysis.
2. **Expanded tissue cohorts**: Larger SLE synovial and renal biopsy collections will improve statistical power for tissue-specific findings.
3. **Clinical metadata integration**: Incorporating SLEDAI and complement data (when available) will enable senescence-activity correlation analysis.
4. **Functional validation**: Flow cytometry and co-culture experiments (see Methods: External Validation Plan) will confirm surface antigen expression on senescent immune populations.
